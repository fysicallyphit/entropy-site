EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The proposed colocation data centre in East London, utilising direct liquid cooling, has the potential to generate significant economic and environmental benefits. The data centre's IT load of 10 MW, combined with a Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) of 1.4, results in a total facility load of 14,000 kW. Of this, 9,800 kW is recoverable heat, which can be sold to a district heating network. This heat recovery process can generate an annual heat revenue of €4,485,558 and displace 30,991.1 tonnes of CO2 annually. The annual carbon credit is estimated at €2,014,423. The gross capital expenditure (capex) for this project is €1,960,000, but with a grant value of €784,000, the net capex is reduced to €1,176,000. The annual operational expenditure (opex) is €23,520, resulting in an annual net benefit of €6,476,461. The simple payback period for this investment is 0.2 years.

METHODOLOGY NOTE

The calculations are based on the data centre's IT load and PUE to determine the total facility load. The recoverable heat is then calculated, taking into account the type of cooling system used. This heat is then sold to a district heating network, generating revenue. The CO2 displacement is calculated based on the amount of heat recovered, and this displacement is monetised through carbon credits. The capex and opex are then subtracted from the total revenue to determine the net benefit.

RISKS & ASSUMPTIONS

- The calculations assume a constant IT load and PUE throughout the year.
- The heat recovery system's efficiency is assumed to be constant and optimal.
- The price of heat sold to the district heating network is assumed to be constant.
- The value of carbon credits is subject to market fluctuations and regulatory changes.
- The grant and loan incentives are subject to approval and compliance with the respective programmes' conditions.
- The calculations do not account for potential changes in energy prices or technology advancements that could affect the project's profitability.

GOVERNMENT INCENTIVES

The project qualifies for the BAFA Bundesförderung Effiziente Wärmenetze (BEW) grant, which covers 40% of the capex. It also qualifies for the KfW 293 Wärmenetze low-interest loan for heat network infrastructure. In the UK context, the project could also benefit from Heat Network Zoning, Biomethane and Green Gas (BNG) certificates, and Renewable Energy Guarantees of Origin (REGO) certificates.
